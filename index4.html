Enterprise Architecture

Enterprise Architecture (EA) is an organizational blueprint that aligns the organization's vision and mission, risk, and Key Performance Indicator (KPI) with IT in the perspective of four domains which are business, data, application, and technology. Recognition of the EA field of study is useful for mapping the structure and objectives of the organization and the four domains. EA is widely used to align business and information technology in an organization. In EA, you will face many types of EA frameworks like TOGAF, Zachman, Gartner, and so on.

IT Service & Risk Management

Information Technology Service Management (ITSM) is the activities performed by an organization to design, build, deliver, operate and control IT services offered to customers that are often equated with ITIL. Meanwhile, IT Risk Management is the application of risk management methods to manage IT risk consists of the policies, procedures, and technologies used by companies to mitigate threats. Both of these fields of study can help for focusing on value, continual improvement, and guide future decision-making. Frameworks for ITSM and IT Risk Management include COBIT, ISO, etc.

IT Audit & Governance

Information Technology Audit (IT Audit) is a form of supervision and control of the overall IT infrastructure. IT audit can run in tandem with financial audits and internal audits, or with other similar monitoring and evaluation activities. On the other side, IT governance is a policy of using IT in an organization or company. These fields of study lead us to evaluate the system's internal control design and effectiveness. Eventually, it is expected that the company will align IT resources with organizational goals and strategies.

Project Management

Project management is the application of processes, methods, skills, knowledge, tools, techniques, and experience to achieve specific project objectives according to the project acceptance criteria which eventually can give value to people. Project management has final deliverables that are constrained to a finite timescale and budget. To be acquainted with this field of study, we need to learn about the fundamental features of project management, including ownership of the business case, engaging with stakeholders, certain types of projects such as Agile, Waterfall, Lean, and much more.
